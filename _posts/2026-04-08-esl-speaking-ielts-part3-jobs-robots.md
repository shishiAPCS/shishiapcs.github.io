## **Q1. What jobs do you think are rather difficult?**

### **答题思路（中文）**

这道题可以用 **“先定义难工作 → 再和AI时代做对比 → 给具体例子 → 最后总结”** 的方式来答。
先不要只说“医生很难”“老师很难”，而是先说你认为什么样的工作算难，比如那些需要很多年才能掌握的工作。
然后可以顺带加一个时代背景，说现在有些知识型工作因为 AI 变得更容易了，但那种真正靠长期经验积累的工作还是很难。
接着举例，比如工匠、厨师这类职业，它们靠长期练习和反复试错。
最后再总结，强调最难的是那些依赖长期经验和技能积累的工作。

### **Version 1 (Natural & Smooth)**

“Well, I think jobs that **require years of practice** are usually quite difficult. Especially nowadays, with the **rise of AI**, some **knowledge-based jobs** might become easier or even be replaced, but skills that take a long time to master are still really challenging.

For example, jobs like **craftsmen** or **chefs**. Their skills come from years of **hands-on experience** and **trial and error**, and that’s not something you can **pick up overnight**. It’s also hard for others to compete at that level. So yeah, I’d say jobs that rely on **long-term experience and skill** are the most difficult.”

#### **Version 1 重点词汇**

* **require years of practice** — 需要多年练习
* **rise of AI** — AI 的兴起
* **knowledge-based jobs** — 知识型工作
* **craftsmen** — 工匠
* **chefs** — 厨师
* **hands-on experience** — 实践经验
* **trial and error** — 反复试错
* **pick up overnight** — 一夜之间学会
* **long-term experience and skill** — 长期经验和技能

### **Version 2 (More Expressive & Idiomatic)**

“I’d say jobs that **take years to master** are definitely among the most difficult. With AI developing so quickly, some knowledge-based work might be replaced, but **hands-on, experience-based jobs** are much harder to replicate.

Take craftsmen or professional chefs, for example. Their skills are **built up over years** of practice and **trial and error**, and you can’t just **learn that overnight**. It takes real **dedication**, and not everyone can reach that level. So I think jobs that require **long-term skill-building** really **stand out as the most challenging**.”

#### **Version 2 重点词汇**

* **take years to master** — 需要多年才能掌握
* **hands-on, experience-based jobs** — 动手型、经验型工作
* **built up over years** — 经过多年积累形成
* **trial and error** — 反复试错
* **learn that overnight** — 一夜之间学会
* **dedication** — 投入，奉献精神
* **long-term skill-building** — 长期技能积累
* **stand out as the most challenging** — 显得尤其有挑战性

## **Q2. What qualities do people need to do difficult jobs?**

### **答题思路（中文）**

这题很适合用 **“两个品质 + 分别解释 + 最后总结”** 的结构。
你的回答里最核心的是 **patience** 和 **resilience**，所以可以直接围绕这两个词展开。
先说耐心为什么重要，因为难工作往往需要很长时间去学，而且过程中会不断试错。
再说韧性为什么重要，因为即使有耐心，人也一定会遇到挫折，如果一遇到失败就放弃，就坚持不下去。
最后总结，说这两个品质结合起来，才更有可能胜任难工作。

### **Version 1 (Natural & Smooth)**

“Well, I think the two most important qualities are **patience** and **resilience**. Patience is essential because many difficult jobs **take a long time to learn**. You often need a lot of practice and **trial and error**, so without patience, it’s hard to improve.

At the same time, resilience is just as important. Even if you’re patient, you’re going to **face setbacks**, and if you **give up too easily**, you won’t make progress. So you need to be able to **deal with failure**, **learn from your mistakes**, and **keep going**. I think with these two qualities, people are much more likely to handle difficult jobs.”

#### **Version 1 重点词汇**

* **patience** — 耐心
* **resilience** — 韧性
* **take a long time to learn** — 需要很长时间去学
* **trial and error** — 反复试错
* **face setbacks** — 遇到挫折
* **give up too easily** — 太容易放弃
* **deal with failure** — 面对失败
* **learn from your mistakes** — 从错误中学习
* **keep going** — 继续坚持

### **Version 2 (More Expressive & Idiomatic)**

“I’d say patience and resilience are key. First of all, patience is **crucial** because **mastering difficult tasks takes time**—you can’t rush it. There’s usually a lot of **trial and error involved**, so you need to **stick with it**.

On top of that, resilience really matters. Things won’t always go smoothly, and you’re **bound to run into setbacks**. If you can **bounce back**, **learn from your mistakes**, and **keep pushing forward**, you’ll have a much better chance of succeeding. So yeah, I think these two qualities **go hand in hand** when it comes to handling tough jobs.”

#### **Version 2 重点词汇**

* **crucial** — 至关重要的
* **mastering difficult tasks takes time** — 掌握困难任务需要时间
* **trial and error involved** — 包含大量试错过程
* **stick with it** — 坚持下去
* **bound to run into setbacks** — 注定会遇到挫折
* **bounce back** — 振作起来，恢复过来
* **learn from your mistakes** — 从错误中学习
* **keep pushing forward** — 继续向前
* **go hand in hand** — 相辅相成

## **Q3. Do you think difficult jobs deserve higher salaries?**

### **答题思路（中文）**

这题最好的答法不是简单说 yes 或 no，而是用 **“部分同意 → 举例说明 → 再加限制条件 → 总结”**。
先说你部分同意，因为有些工作确实很难，而且需要很多年训练和投入，比如医生。
然后别停在这里，还要加一个更成熟的角度：工资不只是由工作难度决定，也和市场需求有关。
这样你就能自然引出 **supply and demand**。
最后总结时说，工资通常是“工作难度 + 市场需求”共同决定的，这样答案就很有层次。

### **Version 1 (Natural & Smooth)**

“Well, I’d say I **partly agree**. Some difficult jobs do deserve **higher salaries**, especially if they require years of training and a lot of investment. For example, becoming a doctor takes a long time and a lot of effort, so it makes sense that they’re **well paid**.

But at the same time, salary also depends on **market demand**. If a job is difficult but not really **in demand**, then the pay might not be that high. So in the end, I think it’s a mix of how hard the job is and how much society actually needs it.”

#### **Version 1 重点词汇**

* **partly agree** — 部分同意
* **higher salaries** — 更高的工资
* **well paid** — 薪资高的
* **market demand** — 市场需求
* **in demand** — 有需求的

### **Version 2 (More Expressive & Idiomatic)**

“I’d say I **agree to some extent**. Jobs that are difficult and require years of training, like being a doctor, definitely deserve **higher pay** because of the time, effort, and money involved.

That said, salary isn’t just about how tough a job is—it also **comes down to** **supply and demand**. If a skill is rare and in high demand, employers are willing to pay more. But if there’s less demand, even a difficult job might not pay that well. So yeah, it’s really about both the **level of difficulty** and **market demand**.”

#### **Version 2 重点词汇**

* **agree to some extent** — 在某种程度上同意
* **higher pay** — 更高的薪水
* **comes down to** — 归根结底取决于
* **supply and demand** — 供需关系
* **level of difficulty** — 难度水平
* **market demand** — 市场需求

## **Q4. What are the differences between difficult jobs and easy jobs?**

### **答题思路（中文）**

这题适合用 **“技能要求对比 + 工作内容对比 + 总结”** 的结构。
先说 easy jobs 往往是入门级的，要求较少，大多数人可以比较快上手。
然后再说 difficult jobs 往往需要专业知识、长期训练和经验。
接着补一个维度：容易的工作很多时候更重复，照着流程做就行；而难工作通常要求解决问题、快速反应、做决定。
最后总结，说核心差异在于技能水平、训练时间和思考深度。

### **Version 1 (Natural & Smooth)**

“Well, I think the main difference is in the **level of skill** and **responsibility required**. Easy jobs are usually **entry-level**, so there aren’t many requirements, and most people can **pick them up quite quickly**.

In contrast, difficult jobs often require **specialized knowledge** or years of training. For example, if you’re a scientist, you need a strong background in your field and a lot of experience.

Another difference is the type of work. Easier jobs tend to be more **repetitive**—you just **follow instructions** and do the same tasks over and over again. But more difficult jobs usually involve **problem-solving** and **critical thinking**. So yeah, I’d say the key differences are skill level, training, and the type of work involved.”

#### **Version 1 重点词汇**

* **level of skill** — 技能水平
* **responsibility required** — 所要求承担的责任
* **entry-level** — 入门级的
* **pick them up quite quickly** — 很快上手
* **specialized knowledge** — 专业知识
* **repetitive** — 重复性的
* **follow instructions** — 按指示做
* **problem-solving** — 解决问题
* **critical thinking** — 批判性思维 / 深度思考能力

### **Version 2 (More Expressive & Idiomatic)**

“I’d say the biggest difference **comes down to** **skill level** and the **kind of work involved**. Easy jobs are often **entry-level**, with **fewer requirements**, so most people can **get the hang of them** pretty quickly.

On the other hand, difficult jobs usually require **specialized skills** and years of training. Take scientists, for example—they need **deep knowledge** and a lot of experience to do their job well.

Also, easier jobs tend to be more repetitive—you’re basically **following a set routine**. But difficult jobs often require you to **think on your feet**, **solve problems**, and **make decisions**. So overall, it’s about the **level of expertise** and how much thinking the job involves.”

#### **Version 2 重点词汇**

* **come down to** — 归根结底是
* **skill level** — 技能水平
* **kind of work involved** — 涉及的工作类型
* **entry-level** — 入门级
* **fewer requirements** — 更少的要求
* **get the hang of them** — 掌握窍门，上手
* **specialized skills** — 专业技能
* **deep knowledge** — 深厚知识
* **following a set routine** — 按固定流程做事
* **think on your feet** — 临场快速反应
* **solve problems** — 解决问题
* **make decisions** — 做决定
* **level of expertise** — 专业水平

## **Q5. Do you think robots can replace human beings in every industry?**

### **答题思路（中文）**

这题很适合答成 **“先否定 → 承认机器人能做一部分 → 举一个不能完全替代的行业 → 总结”**。
先明确表态，说你不认为机器人能取代所有行业的人类。
然后承认它们确实能接手一些重复性、程序化的工作。
接着举一个最有说服力的例子，比如 teaching，因为教学不只是传递知识，还包括理解学生、建立信任、提供情感支持。
最后总结，说机器人可以分担工作，但在依赖同理心和沟通的职业中，无法完全替代人类。

### **Version 1 (Natural & Smooth)**

“Not really. I don’t think robots can **replace humans in every industry**. In many fields, especially ones that involve **human interaction**, people still prefer dealing with real humans rather than machines.

Take teaching, for example. Even though AI can help with **delivering content**, teachers do much more than that. They **build relationships** with students, understand their needs, and provide **emotional support**. That **human connection** is something robots can’t really replicate.

So yeah, I think robots can **handle some tasks**, especially **routine ones**, but they can’t fully replace humans, at least not **in the foreseeable future**.”

#### **Version 1 重点词汇**

* **replace humans in every industry** — 在每个行业取代人类
* **human interaction** — 人际互动
* **delivering content** — 传递内容
* **build relationships** — 建立关系
* **emotional support** — 情感支持
* **human connection** — 人与人之间的连接
* **handle some tasks** — 处理一些任务
* **routine ones** — 常规性的工作
* **in the foreseeable future** — 在可预见的未来

### **Version 2 (More Expressive & Idiomatic)**

“I don’t think so. Robots might **take over some tasks**, especially **repetitive** or **routine work**, but **replacing humans entirely** is **a different story**.

In jobs that require human interaction, like teaching, people value that **personal connection**. A good teacher doesn’t just **pass on knowledge**—they connect with students, support them, and **build trust**. That’s something robots just can’t match.

So I’d say robots can **take some of the workload off our shoulders**, but they can’t fully replace us, especially in roles that depend on **empathy** and **communication**.”

#### **Version 2 重点词汇**

* **take over some tasks** — 接手一些任务
* **repetitive / routine work** — 重复性 / 常规性工作
* **replacing humans entirely** — 完全取代人类
* **a different story** — 另一回事
* **personal connection** — 个人化的连接
* **pass on knowledge** — 传授知识
* **build trust** — 建立信任
* **take some of the workload off our shoulders** — 分担我们的工作负担
* **empathy** — 同理心
* **communication** — 沟通能力

## **Q6. What role can robots play in the car manufacturing process?**

### **答题思路（中文）**

这题最好用 **“具体环节 → 为什么适合机器人 → 带来的好处 → 总结”** 的思路。
不要泛泛地说“机器人很有用”，而是直接点出具体工序，比如 welding、painting、assembly。
然后解释为什么这些环节适合机器人，因为这些任务要求高精度、一致性，而且往往很重复。
接着说机器人带来的好处，比如提高效率、减少错误、提升质量。
最后总结，说它们在现代汽车制造中扮演了关键角色。

### **Version 1 (Natural & Smooth)**

“Well, I’m not an expert, but I think robots **play a big role** in car manufacturing, especially in tasks like **welding** and **painting**. These jobs require a high level of **precision**, and robots can do them very **consistently**.

They’re also really good at **repetitive work**. Once the process is set, they can **follow the same steps over and over again** without making mistakes. So in that sense, robots help **improve efficiency and quality** in the **production line**.”

#### **Version 1 重点词汇**

* **play a big role** — 发挥很大作用
* **welding** — 焊接
* **painting** — 喷漆 / 涂装
* **precision** — 精确度
* **consistently** — 稳定一致地
* **repetitive work** — 重复性工作
* **follow the same steps over and over again** — 一遍遍重复同样步骤
* **improve efficiency and quality** — 提高效率和质量
* **production line** — 生产线

### **Version 2 (More Expressive & Idiomatic)**

“I’m **no expert**, but I’d say robots are **a key part of** modern car manufacturing. They’re **widely used** for things like **welding**, **painting**, and **assembly** because those tasks require **precision** and **consistency**.

Robots really **shine when it comes to** repetitive work. They can **carry out the same process again and again** without getting tired, which helps **speed things up** and **reduce errors**. So overall, they **play a crucial role** in making production more **efficient** and **reliable**.”

#### **Version 2 重点词汇**

* **I’m no expert** — 我不是专家
* **a key part of** — ……的关键组成部分
* **widely used** — 被广泛使用
* **welding / painting / assembly** — 焊接 / 喷漆 / 装配
* **precision** — 精确度
* **consistency** — 一致性
* **shine when it comes to** — 在……方面特别擅长
* **carry out the same process again and again** — 一次又一次执行相同流程
* **speed things up** — 加快速度
* **reduce errors** — 减少错误
* **play a crucial role** — 发挥关键作用
* **efficient / reliable** — 高效的 / 可靠的
